Mattupetty Dam - Kerala's Scenic Wonder

A stunning concrete gravity dam nestled in the picturesque hills of Munnar

About Mattupetty Dam

Mattupetty Dam is a storage concrete gravity dam located in the Munnar region of Kerala, India. Built in the 1950s as part of the Pallivasal Hydroelectric Project, the dam was constructed primarily for water conservation and power generation. The dam stands at a height of 53.6 meters and is 237.7 meters long, creating a beautiful reservoir that has become a major tourist attraction.

The name "Mattupetty" is believed to have originated from the Tamil words "Mattru" and "Petty" which together mean "village where the landscape has changed." The dam and its surrounding areas offer breathtaking views of the Western Ghats and the tea plantations that blanket the hillsides.

Built In

1950s (Completed in 1953)

Dimensions

Location

Engineering Marvel

Mattupetty Dam is an impressive example of engineering excellence. As a concrete gravity dam, it relies on its own weight to resist the horizontal pressure of water pushing against it. The dam was constructed using advanced techniques for its time and continues to serve its purpose efficiently decades after its completion.

The reservoir created by the dam has a significant storage capacity and plays a crucial role in the Pallivasal Hydroelectric Project, contributing to Kerala's power grid. The dam's spillway is designed to handle monsoon overflow effectively, ensuring the structural integrity of the dam during heavy rainfall periods.

Tourist Attractions

Mattupetty Dam has become one of the most popular tourist destinations in Munnar, offering numerous activities and sights for visitors. The still waters of the reservoir provide perfect conditions for speed boating, which is a major attraction. The surrounding areas are ideal for picnics and nature walks, with well-maintained gardens and viewpoints.

Near the dam is the Mattupetty Dairy Farm, one of the largest cattle farms of its kind in Asia, operated by the Indo-Swiss Livestock Project. Visitors can also explore the nearby Kundala Lake and Echo Point, where the natural echo phenomenon never fails to amuse tourists.

Visitor Information

The dam is open to visitors from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM every day. The best time to visit is between September and March when the weather is pleasant. Boating facilities are available at specific times, and it's advisable to check the schedule in advance. There is a nominal entry fee for visitors, with additional charges for photography and boating activities.

Visitors should carry warm clothing as temperatures can drop significantly, especially during early mornings and evenings. The area around the dam has basic facilities including restrooms, food stalls, and souvenir shops. Guided tours are available for those interested in learning more about the dam's history and engineering.
