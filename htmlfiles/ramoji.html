Ramoji Film City - Fantasy World

The world's largest integrated film studio complex and a spectacular theme park

About Ramoji Film City

Ramoji Film City is the world's largest integrated film studio complex, spread over 2000 acres. Established in 1996 by Ramoji Rao, the head of Ramoji Group, it has been certified as the World's Largest Film Studio by the Guinness World Records. It is a complete ecosystem for film production with state-of-the-art facilities.

Beyond being a functional film studio, Ramoji Film City has emerged as a major tourist attraction with its theme park, offering visitors a glimpse into the magic of filmmaking along with entertainment, adventure, and hospitality experiences.

Attractions and Activities

Ramoji Film City offers a diverse range of attractions including live shows, adventure activities, garden tours, and studio experiences. Visitors can explore various sets representing different eras and locations, from royal palaces to modern urban landscapes. The must-see attractions include:

• Eureka - A multimedia show showcasing Indian history and culture
• Spirit of India - A live action stunt show
• Borasura - India's largest screen 4D theater experience
• Filmy Duniya - A tour through various film sets
• Bird Park - Home to exotic birds from around the world
• Adventure activities like zip-lining and valley crossing

Studio Facilities

As a working film studio, Ramoji Film City offers 50+ outdoor locations and advanced indoor shooting facilities. The complex includes pre-production, production, and post-production facilities, making it a one-stop destination for filmmakers. The studio has hosted productions from Bollywood, Tollywood, and several international film industries.

Visitor Information

Ramoji Film City is open daily from 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM. The entry fee includes access to most attractions, though some activities may require additional payment. Various packages are available, including one-day tours and overnight stays at the on-site hotels. The best time to visit is during weekdays to avoid weekend crowds.
