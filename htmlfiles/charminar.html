Charminar - The Icon of Hyderabad

The magnificent monument that has stood as the symbol of Hyderabad for over four centuries

About Charminar

Charminar, meaning "Four Minarets", is one of the most recognized monuments of India. Built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the fifth ruler of the Qutb Shahi dynasty, Charminar is as much the signature of Hyderabad as the Taj Mahal is of Agra or the Eiffel Tower is of Paris.

The monument was built to commemorate the eradication of plague, a deadly disease which was wide spread at that time in the city. According to a popular legend, the emperor had prayed for the end of the plague and vowed to build a mosque at the very place where he was praying.

Built In

1591 CE

Height

48.7 meters (160 ft)

Architectural Style

Indo-Islamic architecture

Location

Old City, Hyderabad, Telangana

Architectural Details

Charminar is a square structure with each side measuring 20 meters (66 feet). Each of the four sides has one of four grand arches, each facing a fundamental point that opens directly onto the street in front of it. At each corner stands an exquisitely shaped minaret 56 meters (184 feet) high.

The structure is made of granite, limestone, mortar and pulverized marble. Initially the monument was so proportionately planned that when the fort was opened one could see all the four corners of the city through each of its four arches.

Surrounding Markets

The area surrounding Charminar is known for its markets and the legendary Hyderabadi pearl and bangle shops. Laad Bazaar and Pather Gatti are famous for pearls, bangles and traditional attire, offering a vibrant and chaotic sensory experience of the old city.

Visitor Information

Charminar is open to visitors every day from 9:30 AM to 5:30 PM. The best time to visit is during the early morning or late afternoon to avoid the crowds and heat. The monument is beautifully illuminated in the evenings, offering a spectacular view.
