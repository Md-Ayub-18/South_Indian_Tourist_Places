Periyar National Park

Periyar National Park and Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in the districts of Idukki and Pathanamthitta in Kerala, India. It is notable as an elephant reserve and a tiger reserve. The protected area covers an area of 925 km², and the core zone of 305 km² was declared as the Periyar National Park in 1982.

The park is located high in the Cardamom Hills and Pandalam Hills of the south Western Ghats along the border with Tamil Nadu. The Periyar River flows through the park, and the Periyar Lake formed by the Mullaperiyar Dam across the Periyar River enhances the beauty of the park.

Biodiversity

Periyar National Park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. The park has a rich biodiversity with over 35 species of mammals, 265 species of birds, 45 species of reptiles, 45 species of fish, and 160 species of butterflies. The vegetation includes tropical evergreen, semi-evergreen, and moist deciduous forests.

Notable mammal species include the Indian elephant, Bengal tiger, Indian bison (gaur), sambar deer, wild boar, lion-tailed macaque, Nilgiri langur, and the Nilgiri tahr. The park is also an important bird area with several endemic species such as the Malabar grey hornbill, Nilgiri wood pigeon, and white-bellied treepie.

Activities and Safaris

Visitors to Periyar can enjoy various activities including boat rides on Periyar Lake, bamboo rafting, jungle patrol, border hiking, and nature walks. The boat ride on the lake offers excellent opportunities to spot elephants, bison, and other animals coming to drink water at the lake's edge.

Guided treks are available for those who want to explore the forest more closely. The park also offers night patrols for a chance to see nocturnal animals. Eco-tourism activities are carefully managed to minimize impact on the environment while providing visitors with memorable experiences.

Visitor Information

The park is open from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM. The best time to visit is from September to April when the weather is pleasant. Boat rides on Periyar Lake are available at specific timings throughout the day. Advance booking is recommended, especially during peak season. Visitors should follow park guidelines, avoid littering, and maintain silence to not disturb the animals.
