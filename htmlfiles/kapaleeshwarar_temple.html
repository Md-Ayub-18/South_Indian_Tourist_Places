Kapaleeshwarar Temple - Spiritual Heart of Chennai

A magnificent example of Dravidian architecture dedicated to Lord Shiva

About Kapaleeshwarar Temple

The Kapaleeshwarar Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva located in Mylapore, Chennai. The temple was built around the 7th century CE and is a typical example of Dravidian architecture. The temple's name is derived from the words "kapalam" (head) and "eeshwarar" (Lord Shiva), referring to the legend in which Shiva restored the head of a devotee.

According to legend, the temple was built where Goddess Parvati in the form of a peacock (mayil) worshipped Lord Shiva. This is why the area is called Mylapore ("land of peacocks"). The temple has a 37-meter tall gopuram (gateway tower) adorned with intricate sculptures depicting various Hindu deities and mythological stories.

Built In

7th century CE (current structure 16th century)

Architectural Style

Dravidian architecture

Gopuram Height

37 meters (121 ft)

Location

Mylapore, Chennai, Tamil Nadu

Architectural Marvel

The Kapaleeshwarar Temple showcases the exquisite craftsmanship of the Dravidian style. The main gopuram (tower) faces west and is decorated with stucco figures depicting various scenes from Hindu mythology. The temple complex covers approximately 1.5 acres and contains multiple mandapams (halls), shrines, and a sacred tank.

The temple tank, known as the Kapaleeshwarar Tank, is located to the west of the main shrine. The temple also features a hundred-pillar hall and a kalyanam (marriage) hall. The vimana (tower over the sanctum) is plated with gold and features intricate carvings.

Religious Significance

The Kapaleeshwarar Temple is one of the most important Shiva temples in South India. It is mentioned in the 7th century Tamil Shaiva canonical work, the Tevaram, and is classified as a Paadal Petra Sthalam, which means it is one of the 275 temples revered in the Tamil Shaiva canon.

The temple is particularly famous for its celebration of the Arubathimoovar Festival during the Tamil month of Panguni (March-April), when the 63 Nayanmars (saints of Shaivism) are honored. The temple also hosts the annual Panguni Peruvizha, a nine-day festival that attracts thousands of devotees.

Visitor Information

The temple is open from 5:00 AM to 12:00 PM and from 4:00 PM to 9:00 PM. The best time to visit is during the early morning or evening pooja (worship) times to experience the spiritual atmosphere. Visitors are required to follow the temple dress code (traditional attire preferred) and remove footwear before entering the main shrine.
