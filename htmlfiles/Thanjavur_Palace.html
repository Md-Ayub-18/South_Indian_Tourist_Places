Thanjavur Palace - Royal Abode of Kings

A magnificent palace complex showcasing the architectural splendor of the Nayak and Maratha rulers

About Thanjavur Palace

Thanjavur Palace, also known as the Aranmanai, is a historic royal residence located in the heart of Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu. This sprawling palace complex was originally constructed by the Nayak rulers in the 16th century and later expanded by the Maratha rulers who conquered the region in the 17th century.

The palace showcases a unique blend of Dravidian, Nayak, and Maratha architectural styles, reflecting the diverse cultural influences that have shaped the region's history. Spread over a large area, the complex includes several buildings, courtyards, towers, and magnificent halls that once served as the administrative and residential center of the Thanjavur kingdom.

History

The Thanjavur Palace has a rich and complex history spanning several centuries. It was initially built by the Nayak rulers who succeeded the Cholas as the dominant power in the region. When the Marathas conquered Thanjavur in the 17th century, they made significant additions to the palace, resulting in the unique architectural blend visible today.

The palace served as the seat of power for the Bhonsle dynasty of Marathas who ruled Thanjavur until the British East India Company took control in the 19th century. Even under British rule, the Maratha rulers continued to reside in the palace as titular kings until India's independence in 1947.

Today, the palace is maintained by the Tamil Nadu Archaeological Department and serves as an important historical monument, offering visitors a glimpse into the royal lifestyle of South Indian kingdoms.

Key Attractions within the Palace Complex

The Thanjavur Palace complex houses several notable structures and attractions:

Saraswati Mahal Library

One of the oldest libraries in Asia, housing over 49,000 palm leaf manuscripts and rare books on various subjects including medicine, literature, and music.

Royal Hall & Durbar Hall

The magnificent audience hall where kings held court, featuring exquisite architectural details and historical artifacts.

Art Gallery & Museum

Houses an impressive collection of bronze statues, stone sculptures, and other artifacts from the Chola period and later dynasties.

Bell Tower & Arsenal Tower

Distinctive towers within the complex that served specific functions in the palace's daily operations and defense.

Visitor Information

The Thanjavur Palace is open from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM every day except national holidays. The best time to visit is between October and March when the weather is pleasant for exploring the extensive complex.

There is a nominal entry fee, with separate charges for photography. Guided tours are available and highly recommended to fully appreciate the historical significance of the various structures. Allocate at least 2-3 hours to explore the palace complex thoroughly.

The palace is located in the center of Thanjavur city and is easily accessible by auto-rickshaws, taxis, and local buses. Basic facilities like restrooms and drinking water are available within the complex.
