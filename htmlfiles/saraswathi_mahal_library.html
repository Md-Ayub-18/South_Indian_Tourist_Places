Saraswathi Mahal Library - Treasury of Ancient Wisdom

One of the oldest libraries in Asia, preserving rare manuscripts and palm leaf texts

About Saraswathi Mahal Library

Saraswathi Mahal Library, officially known as The Thanjavur Maharaja Serfoji's Saraswathi Mahal Library, is one of the oldest libraries in Asia. Located within the premises of the Thanjavur Palace, this extraordinary repository of knowledge was established during the Nayak rule in the 16th century and greatly expanded by the Maratha rulers of Thanjavur, particularly King Serfoji II (1798-1832).

The library preserves and displays a rare collection of over 49,000 palm leaf manuscripts and paper volumes in various Indian and European languages. These include ancient texts on medicine, philosophy, astronomy, mathematics, literature, music, and dance, making it an invaluable resource for researchers and historians.

History and Significance

The origins of Saraswathi Mahal Library date back to the Nayak rulers of Thanjavur (1535-1675 CE), who established a palace library. However, it was during the rule of the Maratha kings, especially Serfoji II (1798-1832), that the library flourished and became a renowned center of learning.

Serfoji II was a visionary ruler and scholar who sent agents throughout India to acquire valuable manuscripts. He also established a department for the preservation and copying of manuscripts on palm leaves and paper. The library was initially meant for the private use of the royal family and court scholars, but was later opened to the public.

In 1918, the library was placed under the protection of the Government of India, and it continues to be maintained as an institution of national importance. The library not only preserves ancient knowledge but also serves as a research center for scholars from around the world.

Notable Collections and Treasures

The Saraswathi Mahal Library houses an extraordinary collection of rare manuscripts:

Palm Leaf Manuscripts

Over 39,000 palm leaf manuscripts in Sanskrit, Tamil, Marathi, Telugu, and other languages, covering diverse subjects from astronomy to medicine.

Paper Manuscripts

More than 10,000 paper manuscripts, including beautifully illustrated editions and European works collected by Serfoji II.

Rare Maps & Prints

A collection of rare historical maps, including the first Indian map printed in India, and colored European prints.

Art & Illustrations

Exquisitely illustrated manuscripts, including the famous "Serfoji's Atlas" containing 86 maps of various countries.

Highlights of the Collection

The library's most prized possessions include:

  • Ancient Ayurvedic texts with detailed medical knowledge
  • Treatises on Indian classical music and dance
  • Rare astronomical works with detailed charts
  • Historical accounts of the Maratha rule in Thanjavur
  • European books on science and philosophy collected by Serfoji II
  • Illustrated Ramayana and Mahabharata manuscripts
  • Works on Tamil grammar and literature
  • Texts on yoga and spiritual practices

Visitor Information

The Saraswathi Mahal Library is open from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM on weekdays, and from 10:00 AM to 1:00 PM on Saturdays. It remains closed on Sundays and government holidays. The best time to visit is between October and March when the weather is pleasant.

There is a nominal entry fee for visitors, with separate charges for photography. Researchers need to obtain prior permission for accessing specific manuscripts. Guided tours are available and highly recommended to fully appreciate the historical significance of the collection.

The library is located within the Thanjavur Palace complex and is easily accessible by auto-rickshaws, taxis, and local buses. Visitors should maintain silence within the library premises and follow the guidelines for preserving these ancient treasures.
