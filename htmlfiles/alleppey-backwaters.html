Backwaters of Kerala - Serene Waterworld

A network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and inlets forming a breathtaking aquatic landscape

About Kerala Backwaters

The Kerala backwaters are a chain of brackish lagoons and lakes lying parallel to the Arabian Sea coast. This unique network includes five large lakes linked by canals, both man-made and natural, fed by 38 rivers, and extending virtually half the length of Kerala state.

The backwaters were formed by the action of waves and shore currents creating low barrier islands across the mouths of the many rivers flowing down from the Western Ghats range. The backwaters have a unique ecosystem - freshwater from the rivers meets the seawater from the Arabian Sea, creating a rich environment for aquatic life.

Unique Ecosystem

The backwaters host many unique species of aquatic life including crabs, frogs, mudskippers, water birds, and animals such as otters and turtles. The area is also home to many unique species of birds like kingfishers, darters, and cormorants. The fish caught in the backwaters form an important part of the local diet.

Today, the backwaters are used for fishing, agriculture, and transportation. The most popular activity for tourists is taking a cruise on the traditional houseboats (kettuvallams) that have been converted into floating hotels with bedrooms, kitchens, and sit-out areas.

Cultural Significance

The backwaters have played a significant role in the lives of Keralites for centuries. They have been the main mode of transportation in earlier days and continue to be used for local transport, especially in areas where road transport is not feasible.

The backwater regions are known for their unique lifestyle, with many communities living along the banks of these waterways, depending on them for their livelihood through fishing, agriculture, and coir-making. The annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race held in Punnamada Lake in Alleppey is a major attraction that showcases the traditional snake boat races.

Visitor Information

The best time to visit the backwaters is from September to March when the weather is pleasant. Houseboats can be booked for day trips or overnight stays. Most houseboat journeys start from Alleppey (Alappuzha), which is known as the "Venice of the East."

Visitors can also explore the backwaters through public ferries, which offer a more local experience. Don't miss trying the traditional Kerala cuisine served on houseboats, especially the fresh seafood and vegetarian dishes cooked with local spices.
