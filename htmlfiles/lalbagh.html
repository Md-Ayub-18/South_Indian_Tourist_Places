Lalbagh Botanical Garden - Bangalore's Green Oasis

A historic botanical garden housing the country's largest collection of tropical plants

About Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Lalbagh Botanical Garden is one of Bangalore's most beautiful and popular attractions, spanning 240 acres in the heart of the city. Originally commissioned by Hyder Ali in 1760 and later completed by his son Tipu Sultan, the garden features an exceptional collection of tropical plants, trees, and flowers.

The garden gets its name from the Kannada words "Lal" meaning red and "Bagh" meaning garden, referring to the abundance of red roses that once bloomed here. Today, Lalbagh is not only a major tourist attraction but also an important center for scientific study of plants and botanical artwork.

Garden Features

Lalbagh is famous for its stunning glass house, built in 1889 to imitate London's Crystal Palace. This magnificent structure hosts the famous biannual flower shows during Republic Day and Independence Day. The garden also features a picturesque lake, several historic monuments, and the iconic Lalbagh Rock which is over 3,000 million years old.

The garden is laid out with four entrances, with the western gate being the most famous one. It contains rare plants of French, Persian and Afghan origin, along with many native species. The garden's systematic arrangement includes sections for annual plants, medicinal plants, aquatic plants, and a bonsai garden.

Historical Significance

Lalbagh has a rich history dating back to the 18th century. Hyder Ali commissioned the garden in 1760, modeling it on the Mughal Gardens that were gaining popularity during that period. His son Tipu Sultan developed it further by importing trees and plants from various countries including Persia, Afghanistan and France.

After the fall of Tipu Sultan in 1799, the garden was extended and developed by various British superintendents. It was given the status of a Government Botanical Garden in 1856, and since then it has been an important center for botanical study and conservation in India.

Visitor Information

Lalbagh is open daily from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM. The best time to visit is during the early morning or late afternoon when the weather is pleasant. The garden charges a nominal entry fee, with additional charges for photography.

The biannual flower shows (January and August) are major events that attract thousands of visitors. During these times, it's advisable to book tickets in advance. The garden offers guided tours, and there are several food stalls and souvenir shops near the entrance.
