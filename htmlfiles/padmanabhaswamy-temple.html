Padmanabhaswamy Temple - Divine Abode of Lord Vishnu

One of the richest and most revered temples in India, known for its architectural splendor

About Padmanabhaswamy Temple

The Padmanabhaswamy Temple is a Hindu temple located in Thiruvananthapuram, the capital of Kerala. It is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, who is worshipped here in the Anantha Shayana posture (reclined on the serpent Anantha). The temple is one of the 108 Divya Desams, the holy abodes of Vishnu revered in the works of the Tamil Azhwar saints.

The temple is renowned for its magnificent architecture, combining the Kerala style and the Dravidian style of architecture. The temple's main deity is a massive idol of Lord Vishnu in a reclining position over the serpent Anantha. The temple gained international attention in recent years when secret vaults were opened, revealing immense wealth and treasures.

Built In

8th century CE (current structure 18th century)

Architectural Style

Kerala-Dravidian fusion

Gopuram Height

35 meters (115 ft)

Architectural Marvel

The Padmanabhaswamy temple features a seven-story gopuram (gateway tower) built in the Pandyan style. The temple complex has corridors with 365 and one-quarter sculptured granite-stone pillars with elaborate carvings. The main shrine is located under the gold-plated dome and has three doors, offering views of the deity's three parts - the navel and right hand from the first door, chest and left hand from the second, and feet from the third.

The temple stands as a magnificent example of South Indian temple architecture, with its intricate stone carvings, massive corridors, and the stunning 100-foot-high gold-plated flagstaff. The temple tank, known as Padma Theertham, is located on the eastern side of the temple complex.

Religious Significance

The Padmanabhaswamy Temple is considered one of the most important Vaishnavite temples in India. It finds mention in several ancient texts including the Puranas. The temple is closely associated with the Travancore royal family, who were devotees of Lord Padmanabha and ruled the kingdom as his servants (Padmanabha Dasas).

The temple follows strict religious protocols and traditions. Only Hindus are allowed to enter the temple premises, and they must follow a strict dress code. The temple is renowned for its festivals, particularly the biannual Alpashi and Painkuni festivals, which feature grand processions with caparisoned elephants.

Mysterious Vaults and Treasures

In recent years, the temple gained worldwide attention when a Supreme Court committee discovered immense wealth in some of its underground vaults. The treasure, estimated to be worth over $20 billion, includes gold idols, diamonds, jewelery, and other precious items accumulated over centuries. The discovery made the Padmanabhaswamy Temple one of the wealthiest religious institutions in the world.

Vault B remains unopened due to religious beliefs and legends suggesting it is protected by supernatural forces. The treasure discovery has led to enhanced security measures and a renewed interest in the temple's history and heritage.

Visitor Information

The temple is open from 3:30 AM to 12:00 PM and from 5:00 PM to 8:30 PM. The best time to visit is during the early morning hours to avoid crowds and experience the spiritual atmosphere. Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the temple premises but can view parts of the exterior architecture. Visitors must follow the dress code (men: dhoti; women: sari or salwar kameez) and are required to remove footwear before entering.
