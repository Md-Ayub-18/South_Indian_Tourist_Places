Marina Beach - Chennai's Pride

The second longest urban beach in the world, stretching along the Bay of Bengal

About Marina Beach

Marina Beach is a natural urban beach in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India, along the Bay of Bengal. The beach runs from near Fort St. George in the north to Foreshore Estate in the south, a distance of 6.5 km (4.0 mi), making it the longest natural urban beach in the country and the second longest in the world after Praia do Cassino Beach in Brazil.

The beach is a prominent landmark in Chennai and is known for its wide sandy shores, cool sea breeze, and vibrant atmosphere. It's a popular recreational spot for locals and tourists alike, offering beautiful sunrise and sunset views.

Historical Significance

The Marina Beach was originally known as 'Madras Beach' and gained prominence during the British rule. The beach road was laid in 1884 by Mountstuart Elphinstone Grant Duff, the Governor of Madras. Several important buildings and monuments were constructed along the beachfront during the colonial era, giving it a distinctive architectural character.

Many important landmarks dot the beach road, including the University of Madras, Chepauk Palace, Senate House, PWD Buildings, and the Presidency College. The beach also features several statues of Tamil luminaries and historical figures.

Attractions Along the Beach

Marina Beach is famous for its array of attractions including the Anna Memorial, MGR Memorial, and the iconic lighthouse. Visitors can enjoy horse rides, camel rides, and taste various local street foods. The beach is particularly lively in the evenings when food stalls serve everything from roasted corn to freshly caught seafood.

The beach also hosts several cultural events and festivals throughout the year, including the famous Chennai Music Season and various beach festivals.

Visitor Information

Marina Beach is accessible throughout the day, but the best times to visit are early morning (5-8 AM) for peaceful walks and beautiful sunrises, or late evening (4-7 PM) to enjoy the cool breeze and vibrant atmosphere. Swimming is not recommended due to strong undercurrents. The beach has adequate lighting and police patrols for safety.
