INS Kurusura Submarine Museum - Visakhapatnam's Naval Marvel

India's first submarine museum and a testament to the Indian Navy's maritime prowess

About INS Kurusura Submarine Museum

INS Kurusura (S20) was a Kalvari-class submarine of the Indian Navy. After serving the nation for 31 years, it was decommissioned in 2001 and converted into a museum on Ramakrishna Beach in Visakhapatnam. It is the first submarine museum in India and all of South Asia, offering visitors a unique opportunity to explore the interior of a real submarine.

The submarine was originally commissioned in 1969 and was part of the 8th Submarine Squadron. The name "Kurusura" is derived from a mythical sea monster. The conversion of this decommissioned submarine into a museum was a remarkable engineering feat, as it had to be moved from the sea to the beach without damaging its structure.

Historical Significance

INS Kurusura has a glorious history of service to the Indian Navy. It was the fourth submarine of the Kalvari class, which were based on the Soviet Foxtrot-class submarines. During its operational years, the submarine participated in numerous naval exercises and played a crucial role in safeguarding India's maritime interests.

The submarine was decommissioned with full military honors on 27 February 2001. Rather than being scrapped, it was decided to preserve this maritime warrior as a museum to educate the public about submarine operations and to honor the service of Indian Naval personnel. The submarine was carefully beached at RK Beach and converted into a museum, which was inaugurated on 9 August 2002.

Museum Experience

Visitors to the INS Kurusura Submarine Museum get a rare opportunity to explore the interior of a real submarine. The museum preserves the submarine exactly as it was during its operational days, with all its original equipment and instruments. Guided tours take visitors through the narrow passages and various sections of the submarine, including the control room, torpedo compartment, engine room, and living quarters.

The museum features exhibits that explain the history of the Indian Navy's submarine arm, the technical specifications of INS Kurusura, and the daily life of submariners. Audio-visual presentations and informative displays provide insights into submarine operations and the challenges faced by naval personnel during underwater missions.

Technical Specifications

INS Kurusura was a diesel-electric attack submarine with a length of 91.3 meters and a beam of 8 meters. It had a submerged displacement of 2,475 tons and could carry a crew of 75 personnel. The submarine was armed with 10 torpedo tubes (6 bow and 4 stern) and could carry 22 torpedoes or 44 mines. It had a maximum surface speed of 16 knots and a submerged speed of 15 knots, with an operational range of 20,000 nautical miles at 8 knots.

The submarine was equipped with advanced sonar systems, radar, and periscopes for navigation and target acquisition. The museum preserves all these systems, allowing visitors to appreciate the technological capabilities of this underwater vessel.

Visitor Information

The museum is open from 2:00 PM to 8:30 PM on all days except Mondays. The entry fee is ₹40 for adults and ₹20 for children. Photography is allowed but may require an additional fee. The best time to visit is during the winter months (October to February) when the weather is pleasant.

Due to the narrow passages and steep ladders inside the submarine, the museum may not be easily accessible for visitors with mobility issues or claustrophobia. Guided tours are available and are highly recommended to fully appreciate the historical and technical aspects of the submarine. The museum is located on RK Beach, which is easily accessible by public transport and has adequate parking facilities.
