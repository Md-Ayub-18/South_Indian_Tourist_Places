Chinese Fishing Nets - Iconic Landmark of Kochi

A historic fishing method and popular tourist attraction in Fort Kochi

About Chinese Fishing Nets

The Chinese Fishing Nets, also known as Cheena Vala, are fixed land installations for fishing located in Kochi, Kerala. They are a type of stationary lift net introduced by Chinese explorers who visited the Malabar Coast during the 14th century. These fishing nets have become an iconic symbol of Kochi's coastline and a popular tourist attraction.

Each fishing net is operated by a team of up to six fishermen. The system uses a mechanical advantage requiring minimal force to lift the nets out of the water. The nets are suspended from wooden poles and are counterbalanced by large stones tied to ropes. This unique fishing method creates a picturesque scene, especially during sunset when the silhouettes of the nets against the evening sky attract photographers and tourists.

Introduced In

14th century by Chinese explorers

Location

Fort Kochi, Kerala, India

Local Name

Cheena Vala

Operation

4-6 fishermen per net

Historical Significance

The Chinese fishing nets are believed to have been introduced by the Chinese explorer Zheng He, from the court of the Kublai Khan. The nets are set up on teak wood and bamboo poles and work on the principle of balance. During the time of the Chinese influence in the area, these nets became a common sight along the coast.

This fishing method demonstrates the historical cultural exchange between India and China. The Portuguese, Dutch, and British who later colonized Kochi also recognized the efficiency of these nets and they have been maintained through generations of local fishermen families.

How They Work

The Chinese fishing nets operate on a simple but effective mechanical principle. Each installation consists of a net suspended over the water by a large horizontal beam which is attached to overhead poles. The poles are leveraged with ropes and large stones acting as counterweights at the opposite end.

To operate the net, fishermen lower it into the water for a few minutes, then lift it by pulling on the ropes. The counterweights help in lifting the heavy net, making the process less labor-intensive. The catch typically includes small fish, crabs, and prawns which are often sold to visitors right at the spot.

Visitor Experience

The best time to visit the Chinese fishing nets is during sunrise or sunset when the light creates dramatic views and the temperatures are more comfortable. Visitors can watch the fishermen operate the nets, and many offer to demonstrate the technique for a small fee. The freshly caught seafood can be bought and taken to nearby restaurants to be cooked to preference.

The area around the nets has developed into a vibrant tourist spot with numerous stalls selling souvenirs, local handicrafts, and street food. Boat rides are available from this location to explore the backwaters and get a different perspective of the nets from the water.
