Wayanad - The Green Paradise

Explore the lush green hills, misty mountains, and rich biodiversity of Kerala's nature paradise

About Wayanad

Wayanad, the green paradise nestled among the mountains of the Western Ghats, forms the borderland of the greener part of Kerala. Located at a distance of about 76 km from the sea shores of Kozhikode, this verdant hill station is full of plantations, forests, and wildlife. The name Wayanad is derived from 'Vayal Nadu' which means the land of paddy fields.

With an array of tourist spots including beautiful lakes, dams, waterfalls, and viewpoints, Wayanad is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Kerala. The district is known for its rich tribal heritage and is home to several indigenous communities who have preserved their traditional way of life.

Elevation

700 to 2,100 meters above sea level

Forest Cover

Over 80% of the district is forested

Best Time to Visit

October to May

Location

Northern Kerala, Western Ghats

Natural Attractions

Wayanad is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty. The Chembra Peak, the highest peak in Wayanad, offers a breathtaking view of the entire district. The Edakkal Caves, with their ancient petroglyphs, provide a glimpse into prehistoric human settlements. The Banasura Sagar Dam, the largest earth dam in India, is set against the backdrop of the Banasura Hills.

The district is also home to several beautiful waterfalls including Meenmutty Falls and Soochipara Falls, which are major tourist attractions. The Pookode Lake, a freshwater lake surrounded by forests, offers boating facilities and is a perfect spot for nature lovers.

Wildlife and Biodiversity

Wayanad is part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve and is home to rich flora and fauna. The Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, also known as Muthanga Wildlife Sanctuary, is home to elephants, tigers, leopards, and various species of deer. The Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary is another important biodiversity hotspot in the region.

The district has several coffee, tea, and spice plantations that add to its natural beauty. The region's climate and soil conditions make it ideal for growing a variety of spices including pepper, cardamom, vanilla, and cinnamon.

Cultural Heritage

Wayanad has a rich cultural heritage with a significant tribal population. The indigenous communities including Paniyas, Adiyas, Kattunayakans, and Kurichiyans have preserved their traditional customs, rituals, and art forms. The district hosts several tribal festivals that showcase their unique culture and traditions.

The ancient Jain Temple at Sultan Bathery, built in the 13th century, is an important historical monument. The Valliyoorkavu Temple is another significant religious site that hosts an annual festival attended by thousands of devotees.

Visitor Information

The nearest airport is Calicut International Airport (about 100 km away) and the nearest major railway station is Kozhikode (about 110 km away). Wayanad is well-connected by road to all major cities in Kerala and neighboring states. The best time to visit is from October to May when the weather is pleasant. Monsoon season (June to September) offers lush green landscapes but some areas may be inaccessible due to heavy rains.

Accommodation options range from luxury resorts to budget homestays and tree houses. Adventure activities include trekking, rock climbing, bamboo rafting, and wildlife safaris. Local cuisine features traditional Kerala dishes with a focus on rice, coconut, and locally grown spices.
