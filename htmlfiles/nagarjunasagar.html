Nagarjuna Sagar Dam - Engineering Marvel

One of the largest masonry dams in the world, harnessing the mighty Krishna River

About Nagarjuna Sagar Dam

Nagarjuna Sagar Dam is one of the largest and most famous masonry dams in the world, built across the Krishna River in Nalgonda district of Telangana. The dam is named after Nagarjuna, a Buddhist scholar and saint who lived in the 2nd century AD, and the nearby Nagarjunakonda island which was a major Buddhist center.

Construction of this massive irrigation and hydroelectric project began in 1955 and was completed in 1972. The dam created one of the largest man-made lakes in the world and plays a crucial role in irrigation for over 10 lakh acres of land in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.

Engineering Marvel

Nagarjuna Sagar Dam is considered an engineering marvel of modern India. It is one of the earliest irrigation and hydroelectric projects in India and was built using 7.5 million cubic meters of masonry, more than any other dam in the world at the time of its construction.

The dam has 26 crest gates and a large reservoir with a capacity of 11.472 billion cubic meters. The left canal (Jawahar canal) is 203 km long and the right canal (Lal Bahadur Shastri canal) is 179 km long, both among the longest canals in India.

Hydroelectric Power

The dam has a hydroelectric power plant with an installed capacity of 815.6 MW. The power station has 8 units (1x110 MW + 7x100.8 MW) and generates substantial electricity for the region. The first unit was commissioned in 1978 and the last in 1985.

Tourist Attractions

The dam site offers breathtaking views and several attractions for tourists:

  • Viewpoints: Several viewpoints offer panoramic views of the massive dam and reservoir
  • Boat Rides: Enjoy boat rides to Nagarjunakonda island and the dam site
  • Ethipothala Falls: A beautiful waterfall located about 11 km from the dam
  • Nagarjunakonda Museum: Showcases Buddhist artifacts and relics from the ancient Buddhist civilization
  • Light and Sound Show: Evening show depicting the history and construction of the dam

Visitor Information

The dam is open to visitors every day from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. The best time to visit is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and the water level is high. Boating services are available from 9:30 AM to 4:30 PM.

There are several accommodation options near the dam including APTDC's Vijay Vihar complex. Permits are required to visit certain areas of the dam, which can be obtained from the dam authorities.
