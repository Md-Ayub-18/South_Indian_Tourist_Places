Kovalam Beach - Kerala's Coastal Paradise

Famous for its crescent-shaped shoreline and lighthouse overlooking the Arabian Sea

About Kovalam Beach

Kovalam is an internationally renowned beach destination located just 16 km from Thiruvananthapuram, the capital city of Kerala. The name Kovalam means "grove of coconut trees," and true to its name, the area is filled with endless coconut palms that line the sandy shores. The beach consists of three adjacent crescent-shaped bays: Lighthouse Beach, Hawah Beach, and Samudra Beach.

Kovalam gained international attention in the 1930s when the Regent Maharani of Travancore built her beach resort, the Kovalam Beach Hotel. However, it was in the 1970s that Kovalam became a popular destination for hippies and backpackers, eventually transforming into one of India's most famous beach destinations with a well-developed tourism infrastructure.

The Three Bays of Kovalam

Kovalam is characterized by its three adjacent crescent beaches:

Lighthouse Beach: The southernmost and most popular beach, named after the 35-meter tall Vizhinjam Lighthouse that stands on its southern promontory. This beach has the highest concentration of shops, restaurants, and accommodation options.

Hawah Beach: Also known as Eve's Beach, this is the central beach that was traditionally the hub for tourists. It gets its name from topless European women who used to sunbathe here in the 1970s.

Samudra Beach: The northernmost beach, which is relatively quieter and less commercialized. It's lined with luxury resorts and offers a more peaceful atmosphere compared to the other two beaches.

Activities and Attractions

Kovalam offers a wide range of activities for visitors. Swimming and sunbathing are the most popular activities, though visitors should exercise caution as the waves can be strong. The lighthouse offers spectacular panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and is a major attraction. Ayurvedic massage and wellness treatments are widely available, with numerous centers offering traditional Kerala Ayurveda therapies.

Water sports like parasailing, surfing, and catamaran rides are available, particularly at Lighthouse Beach. Visitors can also enjoy fresh seafood at the beachside shacks and restaurants, with options ranging from casual eateries to fine dining establishments. The local markets offer souvenirs, handicrafts, and spices, making for interesting shopping experiences.

Visitor Information

Kovalam is easily accessible from Thiruvananthapuram, with regular bus and taxi services available. The best time to visit is from September to March when the weather is pleasant. The beaches are public, but sunbeds and umbrellas are available for rent. Swimming is generally safe, but it's advisable to heed warning flags and lifeguard instructions. The area has accommodation options ranging from budget guesthouses to luxury resorts, with many offering sea views.

Evening is a particularly beautiful time to visit, as the sunset views over the Arabian Sea are spectacular. The lighthouse is open to visitors from 3:00 PM to 5:00 PM and offers a magnificent view of the coastline.
