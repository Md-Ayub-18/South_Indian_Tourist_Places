Kerala Houseboats - Serene Backwater Experience

Experience the tranquil beauty of Kerala's backwaters on traditional houseboats

About Kerala Houseboats

Kerala houseboats, known as "Kettuvallams" in the local language, are traditional rice barges converted into floating accommodations that offer a unique way to experience the serene backwaters of Kerala. These magnificent floating vessels were originally used to transport rice and spices between Kuttanad and the Kochi port.

Today, these houseboats have been transformed into luxurious floating hotels with modern amenities while retaining their traditional charm and architectural style. A journey on a Kerala houseboat through the interconnected canals, rivers, and lakes offers an unparalleled experience of witnessing rural Kerala life, lush green landscapes, and diverse aquatic ecosystems.

Architectural Features

Kerala houseboats are remarkable examples of traditional craftsmanship and sustainable architecture. These boats are built entirely without using a single nail - wooden planks are joined together with coir rope and then coated with a black resin made from boiled cashew kernels. This ancient technique makes the boats flexible and durable enough to withstand the conditions of the backwaters.

A typical houseboat features a thatched roof made from bamboo poles and palm leaves, providing natural insulation. The interior is divided into separate living areas including a sit-out, dining area, bedroom with attached bathroom, and a kitchen. Many modern houseboats also include sundecks, upper viewing decks, and modern amenities like air conditioning.

Cultural Significance

Houseboats represent an important aspect of Kerala's cultural heritage and traditional way of life. The backwaters have been the lifeline of Kerala for centuries, serving as transportation routes for goods and people. The conversion of these traditional cargo boats into tourist accommodations has provided sustainable livelihood for local communities while preserving traditional boat-building skills.

The houseboat experience offers a glimpse into the daily life along the backwaters - from watching fishermen casting their nets to observing women washing clothes by the waterside and children swimming in the canals. It also provides an opportunity to savor authentic Kerala cuisine prepared with fresh local ingredients, often caught right from the backwaters.

Visitor Information

Houseboat cruises are available for day trips (typically 6-8 hours) or overnight stays. The best time to visit is between September and March when the weather is pleasant. Most houseboats include meals prepared onboard by experienced chefs specializing in local cuisine. It's recommended to book in advance, especially during peak season (December-January).

Prices vary based on the size of the boat, amenities, and duration of the cruise. Options range from budget-friendly smaller boats to luxury houseboats with premium facilities. All houseboats are required to follow government regulations for safety and environmental protection.
