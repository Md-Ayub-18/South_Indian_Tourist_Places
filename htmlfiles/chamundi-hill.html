Chamundi Hill - Abode of the Divine Goddess

A sacred hill offering panoramic views of Mysore city and home to the famous Chamundeshwari Temple

About Chamundi Hill

Chamundi Hill is a prominent landmark in Mysore, located about 13 kilometers from the city center. The hill is named after Goddess Chamundeshwari, an incarnation of Goddess Durga, who is the presiding deity of the famous Sri Chamundeshwari Temple situated at the summit. The hill rises to a height of 3,489 feet above sea level and offers breathtaking panoramic views of Mysore city.

According to Hindu mythology, the hill is associated with the legend of Goddess Chamundeshwari slaying the demons Chandha and Mundha. The name 'Chamundi' is derived from 'Chamundeshwari', which means 'Goddess who killed Chanda and Munda'. The hill has been a place of worship for centuries and is considered one of the most sacred sites in Karnataka.

Height

3,489 feet (1,063 meters) above sea level

Chamundeshwari Temple

The Sri Chamundeshwari Temple is the main attraction on Chamundi Hill. The original shrine was built in the 12th century, while the current structure dates back to the 17th century. The temple features beautiful Dravidian architecture with a seven-story gopuram (tower) adorned with intricate carvings.

The main deity, Goddess Chamundeshwari, is decorated with precious jewels and flowers. The temple also houses a magnificent statue of Nandi (the sacred bull) carved from a single piece of black granite. The temple is particularly crowded during the Navaratri festival when special prayers and ceremonies are conducted.

Historical Significance

Chamundi Hill has great historical significance. The massive Nandi statue located halfway up the hill was commissioned by Dodda Devaraja Wadiyar in 1659. The statue is 15 feet high and 24 feet long, making it one of the largest Nandi statues in India.

The hill was also the site where Tipu Sultan, the ruler of Mysore, had a summer palace. The Wadiyar rulers of Mysore considered Chamundeshwari as their family deity and made significant contributions to the temple's development and maintenance over the centuries.

Visitor Experience

Visitors can reach the summit of Chamundi Hill either by climbing the 1,008 steps or by road. The steps are flanked by statues and small shrines, making the climb a spiritual experience. Along the way, visitors can see the giant Nandi statue and enjoy the panoramic views of Mysore city.

From the summit, one can see the entire Mysore city, including the magnificent Mysore Palace. The viewpoint offers spectacular sunrise and sunset views. There are also several shops selling religious items, souvenirs, and local snacks.

Visitor Information

The temple is open from 7:30 AM to 2:00 PM, 3:30 PM to 6:00 PM, and 7:30 PM to 9:00 PM. The best time to visit is during the early morning or evening hours to avoid crowds and heat. Photography is allowed in the temple premises but not inside the sanctum sanctorum.

Visitors are expected to dress modestly and remove footwear before entering the temple. The climb up the steps takes approximately 30-45 minutes, while the drive to the top takes about 15 minutes from the base. There is ample parking available at the summit.
