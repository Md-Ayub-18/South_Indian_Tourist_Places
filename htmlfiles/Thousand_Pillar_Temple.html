Thousand Pillar Temple - Architectural Marvel

A magnificent example of Kakatiya architecture with exquisite stone carvings and historical significance

About Thousand Pillar Temple

The Thousand Pillar Temple, also known as Rudreshwara Swamy Temple, is one of the finest examples of Kakatiya architecture in Telangana. Built in the 12th century by King Rudra Deva, this historic temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, Vishnu, and Surya, representing a unique triad of Hindu deities.

Despite its name, the temple doesn't actually have a thousand pillars but features beautifully carved pillars that create an illusion of countless columns. The temple is renowned for its star-shaped architecture, intricate carvings, and the famous Nandi bull statue carved from a single piece of black basalt.

Built In

1163 AD (12th Century)

Architectural Style

Kakatiya Architecture, Chalukyan style

Built By

King Rudra Deva of Kakatiya dynasty

Location

Hanamkonda, Warangal, Telangana

Architectural Significance

The Thousand Pillar Temple is built in a unique trikutalaya style, with three shrines dedicated to three deities - Shiva, Vishnu, and Surya. The temple stands on a 1 meter high platform and is designed in a star-shaped plan, which is a distinctive feature of Kakatiya architecture.

The most remarkable feature is the beautifully carved pillars, each with different designs and patterns. The temple's Nandi mandapam houses a massive monolithic Nandi (bull) statue that faces the Shiva shrine. The perforated screens and detailed carvings depicting mythological scenes showcase the exceptional craftsmanship of Kakatiya sculptors.

Historical Importance

The temple was built during the golden era of the Kakatiya dynasty, which ruled over the region from the 12th to the 14th centuries. It has witnessed various historical events, including invasions and renovations. The temple was damaged during various invasions but was carefully restored by the Archaeological Survey of India.

In 2004, the temple was included in the UNESCO World Heritage Site tentative list, recognizing its cultural and historical significance. It remains an important pilgrimage site and a testament to the architectural brilliance of the Kakatiya period.

Visitor Information

The Thousand Pillar Temple is open to visitors every day from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM. The best time to visit is during the winter months (October to February) when the weather is pleasant. Special pujas and ceremonies are conducted during Maha Shivaratri and other Hindu festivals.

Visitors are advised to dress modestly as it is a place of worship. Photography is allowed but without flash inside the temple premises. Guides are available for detailed historical information, and there's a small garden and museum nearby displaying artifacts related to the temple's history.
