Hussain Sagar Lake - The Heart of Hyderabad

A sprawling artificial lake that connects the twin cities of Hyderabad and Secunderabad

About Hussain Sagar Lake

Hussain Sagar is a heart-shaped lake in Hyderabad, built by Ibrahim Quli Qutb Shah in 1563. It is spread across an area of 5.7 square kilometers and is fed by the River Musi. A large monolithic statue of the Buddha stands on an island in the middle of the lake, which was erected in 1992.

The lake connects the twin cities of Hyderabad and Secunderabad, and is a popular spot for recreation, water sports, and relaxation. The picturesque surroundings and the impressive Buddha statue make it one of the most visited places in Hyderabad.

History

Hussain Sagar was built in 1563 by Ibrahim Quli Qutb Shah, on a tributary of the River Musi. It was named after Hussain Shah Wali, who was the prime minister at that time and played a key role in its design and construction. The lake was originally built to meet the water and irrigation needs of the city.

In the 1990s, the lake was developed as a major tourist attraction with the installation of the massive monolithic Buddha statue on Gibraltar Rock in the middle of the lake, which was transported to the site with great engineering effort.

Buddha Statue

The centerpiece of Hussain Sagar is the massive monolithic statue of Gautama Buddha, standing at 18 meters (58 feet) tall and weighing 350 tons. It was carved by 200 sculptors from a single piece of white granite and is the world's tallest monolithic statue of Buddha. The statue was installed in 1992 on Gibraltar Rock in the middle of the lake.

Activities and Attractions

Hussain Sagar offers various recreational activities including boating, water sports, and leisurely walks along its well-developed promenade. The lake is surrounded by parks such as Lumbini Park and Sanjeevaiah Park, which are popular picnic spots. The Tank Bund road which divides the lake offers a beautiful drive with views of the lake and statues of famous personalities.

Visitor Information

The lake is accessible throughout the day, but the best time to visit is during the early morning or evening. Boating facilities are available from 9:00 AM to 9:00 PM. The lake looks particularly beautiful during sunset and when illuminated at night. The annual immersion of Ganesh idols during the Ganesh Chaturthi festival is a major event here.
