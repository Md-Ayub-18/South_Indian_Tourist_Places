Tea Gardens - The Verdant Carpets of Munnar

Explore the endless expanse of lush green tea plantations that define Munnar's landscape

About Munnar Tea Gardens

The tea gardens of Munnar are among the most beautiful and extensive in the world, covering vast areas of rolling hills with a carpet of vibrant green tea bushes. These plantations were first established by the British in the late 19th century and have since become synonymous with Munnar's identity. The cool climate, high altitude, and abundant rainfall create perfect conditions for growing high-quality tea.

Munnar's tea plantations stretch as far as the eye can see, creating a mesmerizing pattern of undulating green contours against the backdrop of misty mountains. The sight of tea pickers moving through the bushes with their baskets, the fresh aroma of tea leaves, and the tranquil atmosphere make the tea gardens a truly enchanting experience for visitors.

History of Tea Plantations

The history of tea cultivation in Munnar dates back to the 1880s when the British discovered the region's potential for growing tea. The first tea plants were brought from Assam and China, and the industry quickly flourished under the management of British planters. The Kannan Devan Hills Produce Company, established in 1897, played a significant role in developing the tea industry in the region.

After India gained independence, many British-owned tea estates were taken over by Indian companies. Today, the Tata Group's Tata Tea Limited (now renamed as Kanan Devan Hills Plantations Company Private Limited) manages most of the tea plantations in Munnar. The region produces some of the finest orthodox tea in the world, known for its distinctive aroma and flavor.

Tea Production Process

The journey from tea leaf to the cup involves several meticulous processes. Tea pluckers carefully hand-pick the tender two leaves and a bud, which are considered the best for quality tea. The plucked leaves are then taken to nearby factories for processing.

The manufacturing process includes withering (reducing moisture content), rolling (to break cell walls and release enzymes), fermentation (oxidation that develops flavor), drying (to stop fermentation), and sorting (grading by size and quality). Munnar primarily produces orthodox tea, which involves more traditional methods compared to CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl) tea production.

Visitor Experience

Visitors to Munnar's tea gardens can enjoy guided tours through the plantations, witnessing the tea plucking process and learning about tea cultivation. Many tea estates offer factory tours where visitors can observe the various stages of tea processing and participate in tea tasting sessions.

The best time to visit the tea gardens is between September and May when the weather is pleasant. Early mornings offer the most spectacular views as the mist clears to reveal the endless green carpets. Photography enthusiasts will find endless opportunities to capture the beautiful landscapes, while nature lovers can enjoy peaceful walks through the plantations.

Environmental Significance

The tea gardens of Munnar play a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of the region. The plantations help prevent soil erosion on the steep slopes of the Western Ghats and contribute to the region's biodiversity. Many tea estates practice sustainable farming methods and are involved in conservation efforts to protect the natural environment.

The tea industry also provides employment to thousands of local people, supporting the regional economy. Many estates have implemented welfare programs for their workers, including housing, healthcare, and education facilities.
