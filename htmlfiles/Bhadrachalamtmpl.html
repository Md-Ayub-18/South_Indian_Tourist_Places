Sri Sita Ramachandra Swamy Temple - Spiritual Abode

The sacred temple dedicated to Lord Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana, nestled on the banks of Godavari River

About the Temple

Sri Sita Ramachandra Swamy Temple is one of the most important Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Rama, located in Bhadrachalam, Telangana. The temple is situated on the left bank of the Godavari River and is closely associated with the life of Kancharla Gopanna, popularly known as Bhakta Ramadas, a great devotee of Lord Rama.

The temple is believed to be the place where Lord Rama, along with Sita and Lakshmana, spent a significant part of their exile (vanavas). The main deities are Sri Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana in a unique standing posture, which is different from most other Rama temples.

Established

17th Century (1674 CE)

Architectural Style

Dravidian Architecture

Built By

Bhakta Ramadas (Kancharla Gopanna)

Location

Bhadrachalam, Telangana

Historical Significance

The temple has a fascinating history connected with Bhakta Ramadas, a revenue official during the Qutb Shahi rule. According to legend, Ramadas used government funds to build the temple, for which he was imprisoned by the Sultan. It is believed that Lord Rama and Lakshmana appeared in the Sultan's dreams and paid the amount in gold coins, leading to Ramadas's release.

The temple gained prominence during the 17th century and has since been an important pilgrimage center for devotees of Lord Rama. The temple complex was later expanded and renovated by various rulers and devotees over the centuries.

Architectural Features

The temple showcases beautiful Dravidian architecture with intricate carvings and sculptures. The main gopuram (tower) is adorned with elaborate sculptures depicting scenes from the Ramayana. The temple complex includes:

  • Main sanctum with deities of Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana
  • Separate shrines for other deities
  • Beautiful mandapams (halls) with carved pillars
  • Spacious courtyards for religious ceremonies
  • Ghats leading to the Godavari River for ritual baths

Religious Importance

The temple is considered one of the most important Rama temples in India, often compared to Ayodhya. It holds special significance during:

  • Ram Navami: The birthday of Lord Rama, celebrated with great fervor
  • Vaikunta Ekadashi: Special prayers and processions
  • Sita Rama Kalyanam: The celestial wedding ceremony of Rama and Sita
  • Dussehra: Celebration of Rama's victory over Ravana

Visitor Information

The temple is open every day from 4:30 AM to 9:00 PM. Special darshan tickets are available for quicker access. The best time to visit is during the winter months (October to February) when the weather is pleasant.

Devotees can participate in various sevas (services) and rituals. Accommodation is available in nearby guest houses and dharmashalas. The temple is well-connected by road and the nearest railway station is Bhadrachalam Road.
