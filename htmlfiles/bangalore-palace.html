Bangalore Palace - Royal Heritage of Bengaluru

A magnificent Tudor-style palace reflecting the grandeur of the Wodeyar dynasty

About Bangalore Palace

Bangalore Palace is a royal palace located in Bengaluru, Karnataka, India. It was built in 1878 by Rev. Garrett, the first principal of the Central High School in Bangalore (now Central College), and was later purchased by the Maharaja of Mysore, Chamaraja Wodeyar, in 1884. The palace is known for its Tudor-style architecture with fortified towers, turrets, and battlements.

The palace was inspired by the Windsor Castle in England and showcases a beautiful blend of Gothic and Tudor architectural styles. Spread over 45,000 square feet, the palace features elegant wood carvings, floral motifs, relief paintings, and beautiful furniture that reflect the opulence of the bygone era.

Architectural Marvel

Bangalore Palace showcases exquisite Tudor-style architecture with its distinctive features including Gothic windows, battlements, and turrets. The interior of the palace is equally impressive with its elegant decor, Victorian-style furniture, and magnificent paintings.

The palace features beautiful ballrooms, sprawling gardens, and a ceremony hall that can accommodate over 1,000 people. The Durbar Hall on the first floor is particularly noteworthy with its ornate ceiling, stained glass windows, and elaborate wood carvings. The palace also houses a collection of photographs from the royal family's personal collection, providing a glimpse into their lives.

Historical Significance

The palace has witnessed many significant events in Indian history and has hosted numerous royal gatherings and celebrations. It was the residence of the Wodeyar family, the rulers of the Kingdom of Mysore, who played a crucial role in the development of Bengaluru as a modern city.

In recent years, the palace has become a popular venue for cultural events, rock concerts, weddings, and exhibitions. The palace grounds have hosted international music festivals and events featuring renowned artists from around the world, making it a cultural hub in the city.

Visitor Information

The palace is open to visitors from 10:00 AM to 5:30 PM daily. There is an entry fee for both domestic and international tourists, with additional charges for photography. Guided tours are available for those interested in learning about the palace's history and architecture.

Visitors can explore the palace grounds, the interior rooms showcasing royal memorabilia, and the beautiful gardens. The palace also has a small amusement park for children and several food stalls offering local delicacies. The best time to visit is during the winter months (October to February) when the weather in Bengaluru is pleasant.
