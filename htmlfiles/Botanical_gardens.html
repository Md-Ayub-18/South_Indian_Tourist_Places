Government Botanical Gardens - Ooty's Green Paradise

A historic botanical garden showcasing exotic plants, rare trees, and breathtaking landscapes

About Ooty Botanical Gardens

The Government Botanical Gardens in Ooty (Udhagamandalam) is a stunning 55-acre garden situated on the lower slopes of Doddabetta Peak, the highest mountain in the Nilgiris. Established in 1848, the gardens were laid out by the Marquis of Tweeddale, then Governor of Madras, and are maintained by the Tamil Nadu Horticulture Department.

These beautifully maintained gardens are divided into several sections including the Lower Garden, New Garden, Italian Garden, and the Conservatory. The gardens feature over 650 species of plants and trees, including rare botanical species, ancient trees, and a diverse collection of flowering plants that create a riot of color throughout the year.

Garden Layout and Features

The Botanical Gardens are meticulously planned and feature several distinct sections, each with its own unique character:

  • Italian Garden: Formal gardens with ornamental flower beds arranged in geometric patterns
  • Conservatory: A glasshouse showcasing exotic plants and seasonal flowers
  • Fossil Tree: A 20-million-year-old fossilized tree, one of the garden's main attractions
  • Fern House: Displaying various species of ferns and shade-loving plants
  • Nursery: Where plants are propagated for the garden and for sale
  • Ponds and Water Features: Decorated with aquatic plants and lilies

Floral Diversity

The gardens boast an impressive collection of plant species from around the world. Some highlights include:

Rare Trees: The garden is home to several rare and ancient trees, including the Paper Bark Tree, Monkey Puzzle Tree, and the famous Cork Tree which is over 100 years old.

Flower Displays: Seasonal flowers including asters, daisies, rhododendrons, and primulas create spectacular displays. The annual flower show in May is a major tourist attraction.

Medicinal Plants: A special section is dedicated to medicinal plants and herbs traditionally used by local tribes.

Visitor Information

The Botanical Gardens are open from 7:00 AM to 6:30 PM daily. The best time to visit is between April and June and again between September and November when the flowers are in full bloom. The annual flower show in May is particularly spectacular.

Entry fees are nominal, with separate charges for cameras. Guided tours are available and provide valuable insights into the history and botanical significance of the gardens. The pathways are well-maintained and mostly wheelchair accessible.

Visitors should plan to spend at least 2-3 hours to fully explore the gardens. There are plenty of benches and shaded areas to rest and enjoy the serene environment. A small souvenir shop sells plants, seeds, and gardening accessories.
