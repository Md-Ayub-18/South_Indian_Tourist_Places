Jewish Synagogue - Kochi's Historic Jewish Heritage

One of the oldest active synagogues in the Commonwealth of Nations

About the Jewish Synagogue

The Paradesi Synagogue, also known as the Jewish Synagogue, is the oldest active synagogue in the Commonwealth of Nations, located in Kochi, Kerala. It was built in 1568 by the Malabar Yehudan people or Cochin Jewish community in the Kingdom of Cochin. The synagogue is located in the quarter of Old Cochin known as Jew Town.

The synagogue is known for its beautiful interior featuring hand-painted, willow-patterned, blue-and-white Chinese floor tiles from Canton, China, that were added in 1762. There are several gold crowns received as gifts, many Belgian glass chandeliers, and a brass-railed pulpit. The synagogue contains the scrolls of the Law, several of which are housed in silver cases.

Historical Significance

The Jewish community in Cochin is one of the oldest in India, with roots dating back to the time of King Solomon's trade relations with India. The community grew with waves of migration following the destruction of the Second Temple in 70 CE. The Paradesi Synagogue was built by the "Paradesi" Jews, a name that means "foreigners" in several Indian languages, as they were relatively recent immigrants.

The synagogue was partially destroyed by the Portuguese in 1662 and rebuilt two years later when the Dutch took over Kochi. The clock tower was added in 1760. The synagogue continues to be maintained by the Jewish community of Kochi, though the community has dwindled to just a few members in recent years.

Architectural Features

The synagogue features a unique blend of Kerala and colonial architectural styles. The building has a large, hand-painted clock tower with four faces that show different times. The interior is breathtaking with its ornate brass columns, Belgian crystal chandeliers, and the aforementioned 18th-century Chinese hand-painted floor tiles, no two of which are alike.

The synagogue complex includes four buildings of varying heights. The main hall has a women's gallery supported by columns, and the Torah scrolls are kept in the four-step elevated sanctuary. The synagogue also houses several gold and silver crowns gifted by various patrons and rulers of Cochin.

Visitor Information

The synagogue is open from 10:00 AM to 12:00 PM and from 3:00 PM to 5:00 PM, except on Fridays, Saturdays, and Jewish holidays. Visitors are required to remove their footwear before entering, and men must cover their heads (kippahs are provided at the entrance). Photography is not permitted inside the synagogue. The best time to visit is during the morning hours to avoid crowds.
